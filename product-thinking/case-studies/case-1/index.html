Case Study
Method Advanced Logistics

The Senario

Method Advanced Logistics (Method), a delivery company, was dealing with missing/incorrect information from their Software as a Service (SaaS). The SaaS was taking too long to process new information as well as take in new fields of input.

The Problem

How do we fix the wrong/missing information going to our clients? What solution will have the least impact on our customer service?

Options Considered

Method develops their own SaaS
Find a new SaaS
Develop a hybrid layer - Best Option

Decision & Rationale

A Hybrid Layer would allow for Method to be able to directly receive delivery information from customers, assign it to the correct field in their SaaS, then have it sent automatically until the customer decides to change their data formats. Method would have the greatest efficiency with this Hybrid Layer, as they would have near-immediate value form their data models. The other options (switching SaaS or developing their own) have the potential to have the same issues as their previous one. By switching their SaaS, Method is still entirely reliant on a third-party company with their data. Developing their own SaaS, however, makes no fiscal sense, as the development and maintenence of a whole SaaS is not feasible. Due to this, the Hybrid Layer option makes the most sense. As Method is well-known as a reliable, customer-oriented company, having the shortest downtime and best results will be key for them moving forward.

Expected Impact

Method was given 3 options for their Hybrid Layer SaaS: Option A, a quick timeline but double the cost of the other two. Option B, which was only longer by two weeks, was half the cost of Option A. Option C, the same length of Option B, cost ~20% more than Option B and had no other benefits. Due to the size of Method as a company and their goals moving forward, Option B is the best option for them. It is the cheapest, but with a difference in downtime of only two weeks, Method is far better off going with Option B.

Reflection

This case study is a good exercise in making Information Technology decisions as if I were in charge. Throughout the case, I learned more about how companies have to make large, impactful decisions on the fly while they run into issues. Decisions could also have been easier to make with more information (finances, etc.).
